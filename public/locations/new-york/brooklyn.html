Frequently Asked Questions About Carpet Cleaning in Brooklyn

How long does professional carpet cleaning take?

Most professional carpet cleaning appointments in Brooklyn take between 1.5 to 3 hours. The exact duration depends on the total square footage and the level of staining present in your home or apartment.

How often should I have my carpets professionally cleaned?

We recommend scheduling a professional deep clean every 6 to 12 months. In high-traffic Brooklyn neighborhoods, annual cleaning helps remove the urban dust and allergens that naturally accumulate inside city residences.

Is professional carpet cleaning safe for pets and children?

Yes, our cleaning processes are completely safe for both children and pets. Shiny Rhino uses eco-friendly, non-toxic solutions that effectively lift dirt without leaving behind any harmful chemical residues or harsh odors.

How long until carpets are dry after cleaning?

Carpets typically take between 4 to 8 hours to dry completely after a hot water extraction service. Using fans or adjusting your Brooklyn home's HVAC system can significantly speed up this evaporation process.

Do you move furniture during cleaning?

Our technicians will carefully move light furniture like chairs and side tables to clean underneath them. We ask that you move electronics, breakables, and very heavy items before our Brooklyn crew arrives at your property.

What is the best carpet cleaning method?

Truck-mounted hot water extraction is widely considered the gold standard for deep cleaning. This method reaches deep into the carpet fibers to remove stubborn Brooklyn grime that standard vacuuming simply cannot reach.

How does Brooklyn humidity affect carpet drying times?

High humidity in Brooklyn during the summer months can extend drying times by a few hours. We use high-powered suction and specialized air movers to ensure your floors dry as quickly as possible despite local weather.

Can you remove soot and city pollution from carpets?

Yes, our specialized pre-treatments are designed to break down the oily soot and fine particulate matter common in Brooklyn. Our deep extraction process pulls these pollutants out of your home for a healthier living environment.
